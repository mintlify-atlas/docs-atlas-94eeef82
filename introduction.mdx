Brandon's Matchups is a single-page Next.js app built to track one person's March Madness bracket matchups throughout the tournament. It displays your teams, their opponents, live scores, point spreads, and a role badge (Underdog, Favorite, or Pending) — all on a mobile-optimized card layout that updates itself automatically.

The project explores what hyperspecialized personal software can look like when you pair a simple frontend with AI-powered data extraction and automated sync pipelines.

<Note>
  This is personal, experimental software — not a multi-tenant SaaS product. It is built for one user's bracket pool and is not designed to be a general-purpose application.
</Note>

## Who it's for

Anyone who wants a focused, no-noise view of their own March Madness matchups. Rather than checking multiple apps or refreshing a spreadsheet, you get a single page that shows exactly the games you care about, styled for quick scanning on a phone.

    Matchup data is bootstrapped by dropping a bracket pool CSV into Claude. Claude extracts the relevant matchups, enriches them with tip times and broadcast networks, and formats them into the `Matchup` array in `app/page.tsx`.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Automated score sync" icon="arrows-rotate" href="/how-it-works/sync-automation">

  </Card>
  <Card title="Auto-deploy via Vercel" icon="rocket" href="/reference/deployment">

## How it works end to end

1. **Initial setup** — You paste your bracket pool CSV into Claude. Claude identifies your matchups, pulls in game metadata, and generates the `matchups` array you paste into `app/page.tsx`.
2. **Deploy** — You push the repo to GitHub and connect it to Vercel. The app goes live at a public URL.
3. **Sync** — Codex automations run on a cron schedule. Each run scrapes current scores and spreads, patches `app/page.tsx` in place, and pushes the change to GitHub.
4. **Auto-deploy** — Vercel detects the push and redeploys the app, usually within 30–60 seconds. Your live URL always shows current data.
